Eatin.

Autonomous taxi and food delivery

EATin is a unique service that combines the convenience of food delivery with the comfort of a taxi ride. Our goal is to provide a sense of coziness and warmth to those who are feeling tired and hungry after a long day of work.

In today's fast-paced Asian culture, many people are overworked and in need of a quick and easy way to get a delicious meal. That's where EATin comes in - we bring the food directly to you, so you can relax and enjoy a comfortable ride while you eat. Whether you're looking to satisfy your cravings or simply want to take a break from your busy schedule, EATin is the perfect choice.

Problem

From an experience of being outside hungry and late

I once found myself out exceptionally late, feeling cold and hungry. My only option seemed to be calling a taxi to get home, but even if I made it back, my choices for food were limited to microwave meals or cup noodles since all the restaurants were closed.

Many Chinese internet workers face limited food options after their late-night shifts, as most restaurants are closed by the time they leave the office. As a result, they may skip meals or resort to unhealthy options like cup noodles or microwavable foods, which can lead to health issues. 

Research

Internet workers in China (Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen etc. ) have to work overtime very often. According to investigations, only 10.6% of the workers do not have to work overtime. 45.5% work overtime 2-3 days, and 24.7% work overtime every day.

Furthermore, 31.28% of workers born after 1995 work overtime every day, with 44% working over 12:00 PM and 9.3% working until AM 2-4. (China, TianJin Daily, 2020)

Goal

What if... There is a taxi that brings food to you?

Imagine a small taxi that brings you a warm meal while taking you where you need to go. This innovative service would provide tired and hungry individuals with the chance to enjoy a cozy, relaxing ride while satisfying their cravings. Internet workers often don't have time to find a restaurant to eat at, and their only option is to rush home. To address this problem, EATin combines food delivery with transportation, allowing workers to eat on the go and save time and maintain their health.

The personas help us empathize with the users and ensure that our product meets their expectations, ultimately improving their overall experience and addressing their pain points effectively.

Service autonomous flow

Since the service operates autonomously, it requires organized methods and locations for maintenance. The vehicle's maintenance involves various elements such as battery care, gear upkeep, interior cleaning, and more, which will require a designated garage or space to address all these concerns.

UX Design : User flow and wireframe

The user flow and wireframe I created provide a user-centered design experience for internet workers. The user flow guides users through the product, and allows user to go back and forth to check their journey within the food order process. The user flow and wireframe ensure a seamless experience that addresses the the difficulties to get over effectively.

Prototype

The prototype I created combines different features based on the user's need. It aims to provide an intuitive user experience convenient but without feeling overwhelmed. By carefully designing the interface and navigation, the prototype ensures that users can effortlessly interact with all the features, minimizing any confusion.

Home

The home screen of the APP is designed to display the primary functions, such as the user's location and a swipeable container that can be used to initiate the trigger service at the bottom.


User and settings

On the user and settings page, there are several features available for users to easily manage their account, customize their settings, and navigate through the app as per their requirements.


History

The history page of the app stores records of past journeys, enabling users to review and seek assistance for their previous trips on the service.

Swipeable edge container component

The swipeable container on the edge of the screen is used to display ads for seasonal promotions and services at the bottom of the app. It is also a mandatory step for users to activate the container in order to start using the service.

Pick the journey and confirmation

The prototype includes a journey selection page that allows users to quickly choose their destination and starting point. With options for history destinations and favorite destinations, users can easily make their selection without the need for manual input every time.

Upon selecting their journey, the next page offers a confirmation step. Users can review and verify their trip details to ensure accuracy.

Floating bottons on the order page

The order page in the prototype features two floating components: one to check the journey details and another to sort the available food options. The floating "your trip" component allows users to easily go back to the confirmation page and make changes to their selected journey. The compact floating button design ensures easy access without interfering with the order process. This allows users to conveniently manage their journey and food selection without any disruption or inconvenience.

Order

After confirming the journey, users can select their favorite restaurants or food providers to order their preferred food. The design resembles a regular food delivery application, but users can effortlessly switch back to manage the trip details whenever necessary. 

Choose the time

After placing the food order, users have the option to choose their desired delivery time. This feature accommodates late workers who may prefer to leave late but order their food in advance. It offers flexibility for both users and food providers, ensuring that the delivery aligns with the user's schedule.

Waiting for Eatin. to arrive

After ordering, the user simply waits for the car to deliver the food. There are three steps involved: heading to the restaurant, receiving the food, and heading to the user's location. These steps contribute to the overall arrival time or estimated arrival time.

The prototype includes a map that shows the car's location, the food provider's location, and the user's location. It also displays the upcoming route of the car. This allows the user to track their order in real-time and stay informed about the delivery process.

Scan QR code to get in

Once the car arrives, the user can easily get in by scanning the QR code displayed on the screen of the car. The smartphone serves as a key, with the QR code continuously updating to ensure the user's privacy and verify their identity. This approach enhances security and ensures that only the right person can access the car.

Packaging

Version 01 - Learning from H-point
I purchased a book on car design, published by Art Center, and used it to learn and practice car packaging in relation to my design goal. I focused on the importance of exterior and interior proportions in the process.

Version 02 - Creating a cardboard prototype
For this iteration, I developed the concept of a chair that can lie completely flat. To verify the packaging dimensions, I made a cardboard prototype to examine the inside and outside of the form factor of version 02 from various angles.

Version 03 - Changing the direction
Version 03 continues to center on a side-by-side arrangement for two people, which allows users to lie down almost completely. However, I discovered a significant amount of wasted space at the back of the car, and revising it could make the design more adaptable and serve more individuals. As a result, in version 04, I decided to broaden my ideas and experiment with a face-to-face interior.

Version 03/04 - Surrounding windows
I designed windows around the user to allow them to have a view of the surroundings, which may enhance their emotional experience. This decision also aided in accommodating and expanding the needs of various users, such as tourists.

Exterior design process

I devoted a significant amount of time to experimenting with various surfaces on the clay model. Clay allows for relatively quick surface modification. Using the previous sketches and packaging as reference, the model was created as depicted in the photos.

A radical attempt

I tried a self-operated transformation scheme

Two modes - broaden the range of users

Initially, I considered the possibility of switching between a side-by-side mode and a face-to-face mode, allowing the user to choose between the two options. While the design accommodates different situations, it can also meet the needs of other user groups.

Auto-cleaning transformation

The car is equipped with an auto cleaning system that lifts the chairs to the top and thoroughly cleans the tables and floor of the interior. This fully transformable system ensures a thorough auto-clean before the next customer arrives.

Final design

Enhanced food delivery proccess

Finally, I revisited and redesigned this car as the food delivery process needed to be more efficient for the clerks. Additionally, the auto-cleaning system had flaws that made it impractical.

In this version, I focused more on how food is placed in the car and removed the transformable structures that auto-clean, replacing them with a simpler air circulation system. The seats were also changed to couch-like, stable, and durable designs that are significantly easier to clean.

Key features

  1. The EATin. side screen - The interface on the sides of the vehicle displays the current car status to those outside. It also offers a QR code for the user to scan with their phones and verify their identity to access the inside of the car.
  2. UV light sanitizing - The ambient lighting system transforms into UV lights to sanitize the interior of the car, maintaining it clean and safe.
  3. An out-reaching table through the windows - To enable restaurant clerks to efficiently serve food, a table that extends out greatly simplifies delivery.
  4. Air flow system - The powerful vacuum airflow system effectively removes small particles and food debris from the car and eliminates foul odors from the ambient.
